Revision Prompts
Chapter 6 Guide
  • 1 Clarify the emotional center. What feeling sits at the core of your poem? Tighten images and line breaks so they orbit that center.
  • 2 Sharpen sensory detail. Replace abstract words with textures, temperatures, sounds, and colors that a reader can inhabit.
  • 3 Experiment with form. Try at least one structural change—staggered lines, a repeated refrain, or a visual pattern on the page.
  • 4 Interrogate each line. Ask: “Does this line earn its place?” Cut or condense anything that only repeats what another line already does.
  • 5 Invite surprise. Add one image, turn, or phrase that slightly unsettles the poem—something that opens a new angle on the theme.
Original Draft
Read-only reference
I wake inside a screen-lit dawn, tabs blooming like glass petals across the dark. The world arrives in scrolls and pings, a river of headlines I never asked to drink. Outside, the sky is doing its quiet work, clouds rearranging themselves without a single notification. My coffee cools beside the keyboard, steam writing brief poems I do not read. Somewhere, a bird rehearses the same note until it becomes a kind of faith. I answer emails like small prayers, hoping one will open a door instead of another tab. By noon, my eyes forget the shape of distance, everything flattened into pixels and urgency. I promise myself an evening walk, a slow corridor of trees and uncurated shadows. But the day keeps refreshing, and I keep agreeing to stay.

Revision Process & Submission
Track how your poem changes as you respond to Chapter 6 prompts.
Step 1 · Read & Notice
Read the original poem slowly. Notice where the energy rises, where it falls, and where the language feels vague or overfamiliar.
Step 2 · Mark the Core
Decide what the poem is truly about—its emotional center. Keep that in mind as you cut, rearrange, and rewrite.
Step 3 · Rewrite Boldly
Use the editor to experiment. Remove entire lines, compress images, or add new stanzas that deepen the central feeling.
Step 4 · Prepare Submission
When the revision feels coherent and alive, update the submission preview and check that the poem reads cleanly from start to finish.
Chapter 6 Insight
Craft note
Revision is not just fixing mistakes; it is re-choosing the poem. Each change you make is a decision about what the poem wants to hold and what it can let go of. Use this space to listen to the poem as it changes—notice which version feels most honest, most precise, and most alive.
